Henlo!

Pet Adoption Responsive Website Concept

Overview

Henlo! is a reimagined Pet Adoption Website. Inspired by my adoration for pets but lack of pet-owning experience, I turned to prospective pet owners of all experience levels to create a sophisticated, yet playful experience that can appeal to the masses. My objective was to simply design a better pet adoption responsive web design with a mobile-first approach.

Team: Individual Project

Timeline: June 2020-Aug 2020

Competitive Analysis

I closely studied PetFinder.com and GoodDog.com. Full UX Analysis and SWOT Analysis available upon request. Call outs represent the key problems that inspired solutions for my own pet adoption website concept.

User Interviews

Interview script and key quotes are shown above. The priorities became simplifying the experience, requiring high-quality pet listings, establishing a trustworthy presence, finding the balance between playful and serious, and reducing the pain of the adoption process. Full interview transcripts are available upon request.

User Personas

My interview participants and intended audience ranges in pet ownership experience, activity levels, personalities, and income levels as represented in the User Personas.

User Flow

The user’s journey will often include running a basic search, an advanced search, or taking the interactive pet quiz.

Logged-in users can save quiz results, favorite pets, inquire about pets, and set up email communications—such as notifications when a favorited pet is no longer available or when a new pet is added that matches their quiz results.

Logged-in users will also work toward Verified status when approves them for future adoptions—a solution to the arduous pet adoption process I learned about in the user interview stage.

Wireframes

Low, mid, and high-fidelity wireframes show the evolution of my pet quiz screens from initial sketches to final design. Preference testing helped to finalize the color palette.

Brand Guidelines

Color is integral to overall mood and prospective pet owners will enjoy the muted color palette—suitable for long browsing sessions. Salmon, Aqua, and Red being used sparingly. Typography is friendly and easy to read.

“Henlo!” originates from humorous pet speak first popularized by Reddit. Consider it to be broken English for “Hello!” Full brand style guide available upon request.

Final Thoughts

I’m very pleased with the final solution. It’s approachable, has unique features that set it apart from other pet adoption websites, and it has the right balance between playful and serious.

One thing I would have done differently is I would have sought out participants who belong to various generations so that the Millennial point of view is not the only one that’s considered.

What I would like to focus on next is bringing the concept to life with some animations. On Desktop, I envision the cat’s eyes to follow the mouse cursor. And for the calculating/searching screens, I envision the dog to have a bouncing or skipping effect.
